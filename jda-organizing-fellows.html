JDA ORGANIZING FELLOWSHIP



What is the JDA Organizing Fellows Program?

The GenVote JDA Organizing Fellows program is a unique opportunity to join a national youth-led cohort dedicated to addressing our country’s democratic crises and reimagining a just democracy for all. In the face of a gridlocked Congress and continued attacks on our democracy at the local, state, and federal levels, we are equipping GenVoters with the tools that they need to plan actions, build youth-led Teams and Hubs, and be able to talk to anyone about our generation’s democratic crisis at the local and state level.

The GenVote National Just Democracy for All (JDA) Organizing Fellows program is a paid opportunity for 15 working-class fellows, ages 15-35, to develop as leaders in the GenVote network with 1-1 coaching, training to spark new GenVote Teams and Hubs, and inspire hundreds of young people to fight for our right to vote and a just democracy for all.

apply now

We are organizing young poor and working-class organizers, ages 15-35, to fight for a just democracy for all. Ideal candidates are based in Arizona, Nevada, Florida, Michigan, South Carolina, Ohio, and Texas. It is not required to reside in those states or to be affiliated with an educational institution to apply. No previous political or organizing experience is required, either!
The JDA Organizing Fellows program will run for 12 weeks from August 26 to November 18, 2024
The GenVote JDA Organizing Fellows program is a unique opportunity to join a national youth-led cohort dedicated to addressing our country’s democratic crises and reimagining a just democracy for all. In the face of a gridlocked Congress and continued attacks on our democracy at the local, state and federal level, we are equipping GenVoters with the tools that they need to plan actions, build youth-led Teams and Hubs, and be able talk to anyone about our generation’s democratic crisis at the local and state level.

Through Genvote’s robust training modules and coaching models, JDA Fellows will receive support from experienced organizers to develop their leadership skills, strategize direct actions as part of a national campaign, and recruit young people of all ages to launch local Hubs/Teams that will create an active base of advocates fighting for a just democracy for all in their communities. Most importantly, Organizing Fellows will become electoral justice champions in our movement and will be in a unique community of other badass young people, forging friendships that can last beyond the fellowship.

Yes! All JDA Organizing fellows will be expected to commit 10-12 hours a week and will be supported with a stipend throughout the fellowship (up to $1,600). This time will be made up of weekly community cohort calls, GenVote skill-based trainings and workshops, building and executing direct actions, GOTV, and local organizing activities with GenVote Team and Hub members.

Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis and will close on August 22, 2024. Please email Hope at hmdelgado@genvote.org if you have any questions.
